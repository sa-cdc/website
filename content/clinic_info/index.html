Since 1986--making a difference in the dental health of those in need

Clinic Quick Facts

  • Each year, San Antonio Christian Dental Clinic performs roughly 50,000 procedures, sees nearly 9,000 patients, and provides roughly $3.5 million dollars in free care
  • 41% of all Bexar County Adults haven't seen a dentist over the past year
  • On average, roughly 4.5 million work days are missed each year in the United States because of chronic oral pain

The need is great

Access to dental care can be difficult or even impossible for those who do not have the financial resources to go to the dentist. Minor dental problems become major if treatment is delayed.

Why don't they receive care?

Some are homeless, some are single mothers, others are unemployed, some work yet remain in poverty, others may be recovering from substance abuse, many are elderly or disabled.

Acute Need for the Elderly

Hundreds of low-income elderly and disabled persons live below the poverty level in San Antonio and Bexar County, and most have no dental insurance. Dental benefits are usually not available for seniors in Medicaid programs.

SACDC Offers Access to Dental Care

The clinic provides a wide range of dental services for indigent and homeless persons in San Antonio and Bexar County. SACDC is unique in the area as the only agency providing free dental care through volunteers.

Free Dental Treatment Includes:

  • Exams
  • X-rays
  • Oral Health Education
  • Cleanings
  • Fillings
  • Extractions and more

Clients are asked to pay only for discounted material costs for dentures, bridges, crowns, partials and root canals.

Haven for Hope Partnership

In addition to caring for our community's low-income residents, SACDC provides dental care for the Haven for Hope homeless residents. The clinic, located at the Methodist Healthcare Ministries Healthcare Center at Haven for Hope, includes twelve dental treatment rooms and four oral surgery suites. SACDC remains a separately incorporated non-profit corporation.

UTHSCSA Dental School Collaboration

The University of Texas Health Science Center-SA Dental School 4th year dental students provide general dental care and Advanced Education in General Dentistry residents provide specialized treatment under the supervision of dental school faculty during a weekly rotation.
